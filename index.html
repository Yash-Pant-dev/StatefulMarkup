Thats Cool,
But Why Should I Use It Over
React/XYZ Framework ?

That's a good question. And the answer really is that I'm not trying to compete with React!
Stateful Markup aims to solve different problems.


Let me elaborate on a case where Stateful Markup might be a great option for you-
Say you have an existing vanilla js webpage, that has
over time assumed complex interactions, has a bunch of moving parts, and because of lack of certain abstractions become too unwieldy
to handle. It has relies on complex behaviours, expensive updates, and a convoluted structure.

Stateful Markup is really great here, as you can break down your complex features into components, have a reliable and performant rendering flow, a single source of global state, and simplify existing code using constructs.
The alternative to gain such features is to port the existing codebase to React/XYZ, which is a big time-sink.


In contrast, here you just have to drop two lines of JS scripts, and the setup is done! Stateful Markup
provides the most used features, while still having an extremely small size(15kb!) and fast development cycles due to no compilation needed.
And due to the orthogonal, opt-in nature of SM, you can infuse it where you need it, and keep the rest of your codebase completely untouched!
